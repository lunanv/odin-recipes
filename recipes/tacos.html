Taqueria-Style Tacos

Home

Description

An easy recipe to make taqueria-style tacaos at home. This recipe takes at least 2 hours to prepare from start to finish, but can vary depending on how long you decide to marinate the chicken.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1/3 chicken stock or water
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 16 small corn tortillas
  • 1/4 cup white onion, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
  • (optional) 1 lime, sliced into wedges for serving

Steps

  1. Combine lime juice, olive oil, Worcestershire sauce, salt, onion powder, paprika, chili powder, garlic powder, cumin, and black pepper in a bowl and mix.
  2. Trim excess fat from the chicken thighs and combine with marinade in an airtight container. Marinate in the fridge for at least 2 hours, but ideally overnight.
  3. Preheat oven to 250°F. Place a wire rack on a rimmed baking sheet. Cook for 2 hours, or until the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165°F.
  4. If there is any excess marinade, brush the mixture onto the chicken halfway through.
  5. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes. Afterwards, slice it into small pieces.
  6. Prepare a skillet over medium heat. Grab two tortillas, and dip them in chicken stock. Shake off any excess, and then place on the skillet. Cook for about 30 seconds on each side, two tortillas at a time.
  7. To finish the dish: combine the chicken with the double-stacked tortillas, and top with onion, cilantro, and lime (if preferred).
  8. Serve and enjoy!
